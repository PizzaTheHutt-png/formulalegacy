Formula 1 History - 1960s

The 1960s: Growth and Change

The 1960s were a time of transformation in Formula 1. New teams entered the sport, the technology advanced rapidly, and racing became more dangerous and exciting. The decade also saw the rise of some of the sport’s biggest legends.

Key Highlights of the 1960s

  • 1961: Introduction of the Lotus 25 - Lotus introduced the groundbreaking Lotus 25, the first monocoque chassis car, which would become the standard design for F1 cars in the future.
  • 1962: Graham Hill’s First Championship - Graham Hill won his first world title, becoming one of the top drivers of the decade.
  • 1965: Jim Clark’s Dominance - Jim Clark, driving for Lotus, dominated the season and became one of the most successful drivers of the 1960s.
  • 1967: The First Turbocharged Engine - The 1967 season marked the first use of a turbocharged engine in Formula 1, opening up new possibilities for engine design.
  • 1968: The Tragic Loss of Jim Clark - One of the sport’s most beloved figures, Jim Clark, tragically died in a race, leaving a lasting impact on the sport.

The Legacy of the 1960s

The 1960s saw F1 become more competitive, with new teams and manufacturers entering the sport, and new technologies improving car performance. It also marked the arrival of legendary drivers, and the decade set the stage for the modern F1 era.
